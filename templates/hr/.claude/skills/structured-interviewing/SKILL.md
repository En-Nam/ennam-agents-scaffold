# Structured interviewing playbook

Unstructured interviews predict job performance about as well as a coin flip. Structure is the fix. Use this skill any time you design a loop or run an interview.

## STAR for behavioral questions

Every behavioral question should pull a real past example, not a hypothetical. Probe in STAR order:
- **S**ituation — what was the context?
- **T**ask — what were you specifically responsible for?
- **A**ction — what did *you* do (not "we")?
- **R**esult — what happened, and how do you know?

If the candidate stays in "we" or hypotheticals, ask one more time for a concrete instance. If they can't produce one, that itself is signal.

## 1-4 rubric with anchors

Every question gets a 1-4 score with written anchors:

| Score | Meaning | Anchor |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Clear miss | No relevant experience or active red flag |
| 2 | Below bar | Some experience but shallow / inconsistent |
| 3 | At bar | Solid evidence of the competency at the target level |
| 4 | Strong signal | Exceeds the bar; would raise the team's average |

Avoid 5+ point scales — they invite "3.5 hedging" and reduce inter-rater agreement.

## Calibrate BEFORE the loop

Walk the panel through the rubric and one or two example answers per score level *before* candidates arrive. Calibrating after the fact is rationalization, not calibration.

## Written debrief before group discussion

Each interviewer submits their scores and a short written justification *before* the group meets. This avoids anchoring on the first person to speak (usually the most senior or loudest).

Only then open the group discussion — and start with the lowest-scoring interviewer.

## Bias checklist (run before recommending hire/no-hire)

- Am I scoring evidence, or vibes?
- Did I apply the same rubric to this candidate as the last one?
- Did "culture fit" creep in where I meant "culture add"?
- Am I penalizing this candidate for something I excused in another?
- Would I make the same call if their name / school / company were different?

If you can't answer cleanly, surface it in the debrief rather than averaging it away (Rule 7, Rule 12).
